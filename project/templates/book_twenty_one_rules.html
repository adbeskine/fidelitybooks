An introduction from the author...

Information is everywhere. Future historians will call this period the “information revolution”. Books, magazines, periodicals and of course the internet surround, embrace and even drown us in information. There is probably no topic on which information cannot be found.

If you want to survive the many pitfalls in your office or job, or aim to become wealthy through you own effort, there is no escaping the excess of information once you start to look for it. It seems that everybody from billionaires to snake-oil merchants are desperate to tell you and sell you their so-called secrets to success.

But what use is an avalanche of information to those with an acute need to survive and prosper? You need wisdom, not information, to help you make key decisions. Information‑overload leads to confusion, hesitation and inaction. You can spend time without limit saturating yourself in information, but this does not by itself result in wisdom, wealth or success.

For the past half-million years, spanning the epoch of humanity, people have gone through life making mistakes and if they are lucky, learning from their mistakes. The availability via the internet of all the information in the world will not stop you making the same mistakes that generations before you have made.

But I am offering you wisdom. I am offering you the lessons I have learned. I am offering you the benefit of my experience, built up agonizingly and slowly over a long period of time.

I run a business in London, employ staff and interact with many people. Clients, colleagues, prospective customers, suppliers, manufacturers, delivery companies, local authorities, those who enforce regulations, bank staff, lawyers, insurers etc: the list is endless. In my working life I have negotiated with people ranging from the poor to multi-millionaires in the Sunday Times “Rich List” and from hospital porters to Ministers in Tony Blair's Cabinet.

I have found that there are a number of rules to follow in order to survive in business, at work and in the office. When interacting with colleagues, clients, competitors and others. When dealing with money, contracts and negotiations. When doing deals or simply sitting at your desk and getting on quietly with your job.

Nobody told me these Twenty‑One Rules for survival and success. And nobody is going to tell you these Twenty‐One Rules.
